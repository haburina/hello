What is Crash Course?

We create free, high-quality educational videos used by teachers and learners of all kinds. That's all we want to do. After 200,000,000 views, it turns out people like this. And our videos aren't just for schools; the majority of our viewers, around 60% - 70%, watch Crash Course without being currently enrolled in an associated class.

So far, we've taught Chemistry, World History, Biology, Ecology, US History, Psychology, Big History, Literature, Intellectual Property, and we're in the middle of Anatomy and Physiology, Astronomy, US Government, Kids: Science, and Economics.

Crash Course Hosts

Green Brothers

Crash Course is an educational YouTube channel started by Hank Green and John Green, who are notable for their VlogBrothers channel.

Hank Green

Hank Green is an entrepreneur, musician, and vlogger. He is also the creator of the online environmental technology blog EcoGeek, and the founder of Subbable which was acquired by Patreon. Hank and his brother John created VidCon, the world's largest online video conference/convention. He is involved with several channels on YouTube, including Crash Course, SciShow and others.

(Biology, Ecology, Chemistry, Psychology, Big History, Anatomy and Physiology)

John Green

John Green is an author of young adult fiction, YouTube vlogger and creator of online educational videos. He won the Printz Award for his debut novel, Looking for Alaska, and his novel, The Fault in Our Stars, debuted at number 1 on The New York Times Best Seller list in January 2012. In the summer of 2015, his novel Paper Towns was released as a motion picture. In 2014, Green was included in Time magazine's list of the 100 most influential people in the world.[5]

(World History, English Literature, U.S. History, Big History)

Emily Graslie

Emily is a science communicator and YouTube educator. She started volunteering at the Philip L. Wright Zoological Museum at the University of Montana in 2011. After appearing in a VlogBrothers video by Hank Green in 2012, she was asked to join the Nerdfighter network. She currently stars in her own educational YouTube channel called "The Brain Scoop." Graslie is now employed by the Field Museum as their first-ever Chief Curiosity Correspondent.

Phil Plait

Phil Plait, also known as The Bad Astronomer, is an astronomer, skeptic, writer and popular science blogger. Plait has worked as part of the Hubble Space Telescope team, images and spectra of astronomical objects, as well as engaging in public outreach advocacy for NASA missions. He has written two books, Bad Astronomy and Death from the Skies. He has also appeared in several science documentaries, including Phil Plait's Bad Universe on the Discovery Channel. From August 2008 through 2009, he served as President of the James Randi Educational Foundation.
